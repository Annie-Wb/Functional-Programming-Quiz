# Quiz 02 - Some Theory

Exercises

1. Categories and functors
   - State what a functor is in the categorical sense.
   - Give one Haskell example that fits the idea of a functor.

2. Monads and structure
   - Explain informally why monads are useful for structuring programs.
   - Relate monads to sequential composition.

3. Recursive functions
   - Give a recursive definition of a simple function over natural numbers.
   - Explain why recursive definitions are central in functional programming.

4. Computability themes
   - State one reason lambda calculus is important in the theory of computation.
   - Explain why a small set of primitives can still be computationally complete.
